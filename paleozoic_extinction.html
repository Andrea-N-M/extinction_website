The Ordovician-Silurian Extinction

The first mass extinction, also known as The late Ordovician Extinction, happened around 443 million years ago. This extinction 80-85% of the animals at that time to die. The main cause of these extinction was animals not adapting fast enough to the changes in Earth's climate.

There were two events in this extinction. The first one was an ice age on the entire earth, specifically encompassing the supercontinent Gondwana, which resulted in sea levels dropping. The second wave of extinctions was caused by the end of the ice age from global warming, and sea levels rose again.
